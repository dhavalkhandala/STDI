Herpes Simplex Virus

######
Introduction
Herpes simplex virus is double stranded DNA. It has 2 main types:
@@@@@@
HSV1:- Primarily transmitted by non veneral routes particularly following contact with infected saliva. It is responsible for facial and oropharyngeal infections like herpetic gingivostomatitis and the common cold sore or fever blister.
@@@@@@
HSV2:- Is usually transmitted by veneral route through sexual partner, or maternally to the new born during vaginal delivery.It is responsible for genital herpes and neonatal infection and it also associated carcinoma of the cervix

######
Sign And Symptoms
The first attack is severe with pain, vulvitis, lymphadenopathy, and discharge
Subsquent attack are normally shorter and less severe

######
Test And Diagnosis
Culture of the serum collected from vesicles by aspiration or by swabbing base of the ulcer.
Serum anti-HSV antibody levels are increased with both type-1, and type-2

######
Treatment
@@@@@@
Treatment is mainly supportive with analgesic
@@@@@@
Acyclovir 400 mg orally three times a day for 7-10 days OR
@@@@@@
Acyclovir 200 mg orally five times a day for 7-10 days OR
@@@@@@
Valacyclovir 1 g orally twice a day for 7-10 days OR
@@@@@@
Famciclovir 250 mg orally three times a day for 7-10 days
* Treatment can be extended if healing is incomplete after 10 days of therapy
